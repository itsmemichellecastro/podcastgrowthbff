B

Brand Clarity

Positioning · Niche · Transformation Promise · Authority

1. If someone lands on your podcast for the first time, it is immediately clear who it is for and what they will gain.

Think about your show description, tagline, and first 60 seconds of your trailer or most recent episode.

2. My podcast has a specific, defined audience — not just "Christian women."

Could you describe her in one sentence? Her specific season, struggle, or identity?

3. My podcast has a clear transformation promise — I can articulate what she walks away with after listening.

Not just topics you cover, but the actual shift that happens for her.

4. My podcast is meaningfully different from others in the same space. I can articulate what makes it distinct.

If someone compared your show to three others in your niche, would yours stand out — or blend in?

5. My host bio and show description reflect someone who knows what she carries — not someone still asking permission to say it.

Read your bio out loud. Does it sound like authority or apology?

O

Organic Reach

Discoverability · Keywords · Title Structure · Visibility

6. My episode titles are written for searchability — not just cleverness or inspiration.

Would a woman searching for help with your topic find your episode by its title?

7. I use intentional keywords in my episode titles, show notes, and podcast description.

Keywords are the words and phrases your ideal listener is actually typing into a search bar.

8. My podcast cover art is readable at thumbnail size and communicates the show's niche clearly.

Shrink your cover art to 60x60px in your mind. Can you still read the title? Does it signal what the show is about?

9. My show notes go beyond a summary — they include searchable keywords and context that help the episode get found.

Show notes are SEO real estate. Are yours working or are they one paragraph copied from your intro?

10. I know my Listen Notes ranking or score and understand what it signals about my show's visibility.

Listen Notes is a searchable podcast directory. Your ranking is a discoverability signal worth tracking.

L

Lead Path

CTA Clarity · Funnel Alignment · Email Capture

11. Every episode has one clear, specific call to action — and I say it every time.

Not five things she could do. One thing. Consistently. Every episode.

12. I have a free opt-in that is directly connected to the topic of my podcast and compels her to subscribe.

Does your freebie feel like a natural next step after listening — or an afterthought?

13. When someone subscribes to my email list, they receive a nurture sequence that builds relationship and moves them closer to my offer.

Or do they get a confirmation email and then silence?

14. My podcast listener has a clear path from casual listener → email subscriber → community member or buyer.

Can you trace the exact steps she would take? Or does the path go cold after she hits follow?

15. My CTA language feels like an invitation, not an obligation. It reflects the value she receives — not just what I want her to do.

Read your CTA out loud. Does it make her want to take action — or feel like a checkbox?

D

Durability

Sustainability · Publishing Consistency · Pillar Structure

16. I publish on a consistent schedule and have maintained that rhythm for at least the past 60 days.

Consistency is an algorithm signal and a trust signal. Both matter.

17. My podcast has 3–4 defined content pillars that all episodes fall under — and I can name them right now.

Pillars create a content system. Without them, you're improvising every week.

18. My current content format and publishing schedule is sustainable for my actual life — not aspirational.

What you can maintain matters more than what impresses. Burnout ends podcasts.

19. My episodes are aligned with my core mission — not drifting toward topics that perform but pull me off message.

Have you been chasing trends or what gets downloads instead of staying in your lane?

20. I have a realistic plan for the next 90 days of content — I know what I'm recording and approximately when.

A content plan removes the weekly decision fatigue that slowly kills podcasts.
